Decagon · cash-in side · public sources only

Decagon's pitch is AI for your customers. The cash-in side of their own enterprise contracts is one human in an inbox.

Decagon is the lead testimonial in Campfire's Series B announcement. Ryan Ang's quote covers reconciliation, anomaly flagging, report drafting. What the quote doesn't cover, and what we couldn't find anywhere on Decagon's public footprint, is anything that automates the gap between "invoice issued" and "cash in bank" on a $400K ACV that has gone past due. So we went looking. This is what we found, channel by channel.

-- What an enterprise customer experiences --

Imagine a Hertz or Notion AP analyst, day 30 past due on a $400K Decagon contract. What channels do they actually have?

We walked the public footprint the way an Accounts Payable team would. Homepage, footer, contact page, privacy policy, security page, then the third-party records (ZoomInfo, Crunchbase, Vendr). Here is every channel they could try, and what they would find at each one.

Phone
None published
No billing or AR phone line on the website, in the privacy policy, on the security page, in ZoomInfo, or in Crunchbase. There is no general company switchboard either. An AP team that wants to call Decagon about an invoice has nowhere to dial.
Customer portal
None disclosed publicly
No self-serve invoice portal, no public billing portal, no payment portal. The only "portal" a customer can reach from the public site is the Decagon product itself, which is the AI support platform sold to them. For their own invoices, no public portal exists.
Email
2 shared inboxes only
The only customer-facing emails publicly disclosed are support@decagon.ai (mixed product + tech) and privacy@decagon.ai (compliance). No billing@, no ar@, no accounting@, no finance@. An AP team must guess: send the past-due chase to support@ and hope the queue routes it to a human who can wire the right person.
Mailing address
1 listed
The privacy policy lists one mailing address: 2261 Market Street, STE 5378, San Francisco, CA 94114. A WeWork-style suite. Useful for legal service of process. Not the channel an AP team uses to chase a past-due contract in 2025.
The net effect
Black box
A Hertz or Notion AP analyst, 30 days past due on a $400K Decagon contract, has one realistic channel: send an email to support@decagon.ai and wait. No phone callback path. No portal to log in and view the invoice or dispute it. No dedicated AR alias. The cadence stays private inside Decagon's sales-led customer-success thread, which is why the public footprint is silent. The black box is itself the finding. AR happens behind closed doors at every Campfire flagship customer, and Decagon is the cleanest example.
The irony

Decagon sells "AI handles your customers' support" to Hertz, Notion, Chime. When their own customers want to ask about an invoice, they get the same channel-poor experience the Decagon product is supposed to fix.

Decagon's homepage promise is multi-channel, always-on, instant-resolution AI agents for the customer support function. That is a beautiful pitch and it works. The customer list speaks for itself.

The cash-in side of their own enterprise contracts runs the way customer support ran in 2010: one shared inbox, no phone, no portal, polite chase then a louder one. The exact shape of the problem they sell against.

This is not a criticism. It is the same gap that exists at almost every fast-growing AI company: the close gets automated first (that is where Campfire shines), the cash-in chase keeps living in a private email thread because nobody has built the equivalent of Decagon for AR. We have. That is why we sent this.

-- Where we pick up --

We don't replace Campfire. We extend the loop on the AR side.

Campfire closes the books. We collect the cash that should have already been in them. We start at past-due, end at cash-in-bank, and we work phone, SMS, and email at the same time. Not just one. The loop runs end to end and reports every resolution and every cash event back to whatever system of record the customer is on (which is increasingly Campfire).

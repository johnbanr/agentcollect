-- A 60-day walk --

When a Replit Teams or enterprise customer goes 60 days past due, what does the channel actually look like?

we walked it the way a customer would. three channels. each one mapped to what's actually published on Replit's site, help center, and commercial agreement.

Phone
No phone line published.

No billing or AR phone number is published on the site, the Help Center, the BBB profile, or Crunchbase. The published support policy is email-only.

BBB profile · Replit Help Center
Self-serve portal
Stripe portal for upcoming. Nothing for past-due.

The Stripe Customer Portal handles upcoming charges, plan changes, and card updates. Enterprise invoicing is "custom" -- emailed PDF -- with no portal where an AP team can self-serve pay an overdue invoice. The commercial agreement publishes 1.5%/month past-due interest and "other recovery actions," but doesn't expose where to pay.

Replit Commercial Agreement
Email
One shared inbox. Product, tech, billing, AR.

All customer email -- product bugs, deploy issues, refunds, subscription disputes, enterprise invoice questions -- routes to support@replit.com. No billing@, no ar@, no finance@ alias is published. Past-due AR competes for triage time with deploy outages.

-- Where we pick up --

We don't replace Campfire. We extend the loop.

Campfire closes the books on Replit's revenue. We collect the cash that should have already been in them. We start at past-due, end at cash-in-bank, and we run phone, SMS, and email at the same time -- not just one. Resolution and the cash event flow back to the system of record (which is increasingly Campfire).

Step 01
Invoice issued
Stripe (self-serve) or custom PDF (enterprise). Out of scope for both of us.
Step 02 -- Campfire
Close, reconcile, report
Revenue waterfall, journal entries, anomaly flagging. The 20x case study.
Step 03 -- Respaid
Past-due chase to cash-in-bank
Phone, SMS, email at the same time. Dispute handling, payment plans, escalation.
Step 04
Reported back
Resolution and cash event flow back to Campfire and the GL.

The broken world

CGM had tried everything. Nothing worked.

In-house AR teams. Outsourcing in Mexico. Outsourcing in the Philippines. A dozen-plus traditional collection agencies. A SAP migration that broke card and ACH collection right as COVID was squeezing healthcare providers' cash flow. A perfect storm, a backlog higher than normal, and not one of the legacy playbooks moved the needle.

In Benedikt's own words, the structural problem with legacy agencies isn't effort — it's incentives. Low-paid agents on high variable comp go for the quickest wins. A hundred accounts? They call the easy twenty and push back on the rest. Their income depends on it.

"They're stuck in the 70s and 80s. No dashboards, no visibility. And honestly — I think they don't want to show it."
— Benedikt Brueckle, on the legacy collection industry

The result

$1.5M recovered — including invoices CGM was about to write off.

Over 24 months, this partnership recovered more than $1.5M across 2,000+ accounts. Roughly 80% of every month's recovery lands inside the first three weeks — a window legacy agencies almost never close in.

A chunk of that recovery came from balances so old CGM's finance team had considered writing them off. The mechanism is boring in retrospect: lower-friction payment paths (text, email, one-tap Apple Pay) delivered at the exact minute the debtor is ready to pay.

"You sit in front of your TV, you get the text, you're like 'oh shit, I forgot these 200 bucks,' click Apple Pay, done. AI can do that for 2,000 accounts in parallel. A human collector can only time one call per day."
— Benedikt Brueckle
What we actually did

Six operational moves no legacy agency can match

100%

Coverage, no cherry-picking

Every account gets the same attention — the $50 one and the $50,000 one. Legacy reps triage for commission. AI doesn't.

24/7

After-hours payments captured

Up to 40% of debtor payments happen after business hours. AgentCollect answers inbound at 10pm, takes the card, books the payment. Legacy voicemail can't.

T+hrs

Feature requests shipped same day

CGM asks for a report change, a new workflow, a tweak — it's in production within hours. Their words: "literally no time."

Real-time

Live dashboard on every account

Every call, text, email, open, and click — visible to CGM in real time. No more PDFs, no more "trust us."

Per-acct

Send-time optimized per payer

Every account is scheduled individually based on its own engagement signals. We stopped blasting at "Tuesday 2pm" three quarters ago — per-payer timing consistently beats broadcast sends.

Pre-sim

Every dispute rehearsed before a live call

Before the first real phone ring, AI pre-simulates thousands of possible objections for that account. When the debtor objects, the response is already loaded.

The next bet

Now he trusts us with patients.

24 months of B2B recovery built the trust for what comes next: a B2C patient AR pilot on Virtuox, one of CGM's largest customers — 14,000 calls a month at full scale. A brand new category. Higher sensitivity. Real patients.

"Virtuox is one of our largest customers. We are very sensitive about them. The trust we put in AgentCollect cannot be underestimated."
— Benedikt Brueckle

The rollout is deliberately staged — a small segment first, thousands of disputes pre-simulated before the first live call, scale once the pattern is proven. The softer B2C playbook reflects the reality that half the time, the person on the other end is sick, not stingy.

The Interview

Two years in, the first healthcare IT CFO to go AI-native

The Problem

A perfect storm

Before AgentCollect, CGM had tried almost every collection model in the playbook — and watched each one hit a wall.

In-house AR teams. Outsourcing partners in Mexico. Outsourcing partners in the Philippines. More than a dozen traditional collection agencies. And in the middle of it, a migration to SAP that temporarily broke the company's ability to take credit cards and ACH from its own customers — right as COVID was squeezing cash flow for healthcare providers.

"You had a dysfunctional AR operation, plus system breakdowns from the migration. The perfect storm led to an accumulation of AR higher than normal. That's when I got on the web to find what was out there."
— Benedikt Brueckle

The research was manual — pre-ChatGPT. Days of digging. The goal was deliberate: not another collection agency. Something fundamentally different.

The Insight

Why traditional collections fail on healthcare AR

Benedikt's read on why legacy agencies underperform on portfolios like CGM's — where invoices range from $50 to $500,000 — is structural, not effort-based.

"A traditional collection agency works with low-paid people on high variable compensation. They go for the quickest win. Give them a hundred accounts — he calls the easy ones and gives up fast on the hard ones. His income depends on taking the quick wins and pushing back on the hard ones."

AI inverts that incentive. An agent doesn't play favorites between a $50 balance and a $5,000 balance. It doesn't have a good day or a bad day. It doesn't get tired at 5pm.

"AI is unbiased. You give it 100 accounts — it calls, texts, emails, and stays on all of them. That's the main advantage of having an unemotional, unbiased agent compared to a collection guy who's only after his next paycheck."
— Benedikt Brueckle
The Results

What two years looked like

$1.5M+

Recovered across 2,000+ accounts over 24 months — including balances CGM had nearly written off.

~80% in 20 days

Most payments land inside the first three weeks — a window traditional agencies rarely close in.

Less haggling

Full-amount collection rates improve because AI doesn't negotiate discounts to hit a commission target.

The mechanism is boring in retrospect: lower-friction payment paths (text, email, one-tap Apple Pay) delivered at the moment the debtor is actually ready to pay, not when a human agent happens to be at their desk.

"It's non-disruptive. You sit in front of your TV, you get the text, you're like 'oh shit, I forgot to pay these 200 bucks,' click Apple Pay, done. AI can scale and time it perfectly — a human collector can only make one call at the perfect time per day."
— Benedikt Brueckle
The New Chapter

From B2B AR to patient AR

Two years of B2B collection results built enough trust for what comes next: a B2C patient AR pilot on Virtuox, one of CGM's largest customers. 14,000 calls per month at full scale. A brand-new category — and a meaningfully more sensitive one.

"Respaid being a trustworthy partner — but also very agile — gives you the confidence that there's very little that can't be achieved in this partnership. Virtuox is one of our largest customers. The trust we put in you guys cannot be underestimated."

The approach is deliberately staged: not 14,000 calls on day one. A small segment first. Thousands of disputes pre-simulated before the first live call. Scale once the pattern is proven — adapt if it isn't.

The Vision

End-to-end RCM automation in 24 months

"Within the next 12 to 24 months, revenue cycle management really should be fully AI-driven end-to-end. There's no reason the doctor can't see a patient, push a button, and the claim goes out the door. Sixty to seventy percent will be our own tools. Thirty percent augmented with third-party experts — voice AI collections is one of them. AgentCollect is an important part of this journey."

For a healthcare IT group with tens of thousands of practice customers, the bet is structural: the first RCM platform to fully automate gets a compounding cost and speed advantage in a market that's still mostly manual.

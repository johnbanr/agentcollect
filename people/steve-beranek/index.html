Tell us a bit about your career. Forty years is a long time in one industry.

A career spent inside the collections function

"In my last role at Amazon, I was personally responsible for global bankruptcy and debt recovery. Pretty much anything that was considered late-stage collections, or customers that had already been written off."

"I spent my entire 40 years in collections. About a quarter of that time at Citibank, a quarter at GE Capital, then American Express, Bank of America, and finally Amazon. I'm speaking only for myself here -- not for any of those companies."

When you look at how traditional collection agencies operate today, what's the structural problem?

On the structural problem with traditional agencies

"If you're a collector, your tendency is to focus on the larger balance accounts. Collection agencies target their collectors on dollars collected. So as a collector, you put more emphasis on the bigger accounts and less on the lower balance accounts."

"AgentCollect is indifferent. It doesn't care what the balance is. It works every account the same way."
Steve Beranek

"And because collectors are motivated by money, they take shortcuts. From a creditor perspective, where reputational risk is high, you do risk that collectors will do things that aren't treating the customer correctly in order to get paid."

A lot of debt gets written off. Walk us through Day 180 -- why does that happen?

On Day 180 and why accounts get written off

"180 days is when banks have to write balances off as bad debt. So people work backwards from there. They keep the account internally for about six months, then they send it to a third-party collection agency."

"The agency collector has 200 accounts on their desk. The only way to get new accounts is to get rid of old ones. Either the customer pays, or you write it off. That's the math driving the system."

Where does AgentCollect actually help inside that machine?

The 80% nobody should be touching by hand

"There are a lot of mundane tasks collectors perform every day. The customer needs a copy of an invoice. The customer says 'I ordered 50 items, I only got 45 of them.' These are repetitive things AgentCollect can handle."

"You put your collectors on the customers that genuinely can't pay. The difficult accounts. The ones that need real escalation. AgentCollect handles the rest. That's where your collectors should spend their time."

If you had to pick one thing AgentCollect does that humans can't, what would it be?

The single most important capability

"Finding the contacts is the most important piece of all."

"If I have the right contact, and the customer has the ability to pay, I'm more than likely going to collect. The accounts that don't get collected are the ones where I don't have the right contact. The email bounces. The phone number is missing."
Steve Beranek

"AgentCollect can work all those accounts at the same time and find that information. A collector doesn't have the time. They can't sit there and dig for the right person on 200 accounts. AgentCollect can. That's where it really helps."

What surprised you most when you actually deployed it on a real portfolio?

The result that surprised him

"I was pleasantly surprised. AgentCollect overshot the expectations."

"On the pilot I ran, the internal expectation was around 20% recovery. Honestly nobody expected much, because none of these accounts had usable contacts. With AgentCollect I personally watched it land over 50%. That's not a marketing number. That's what I saw."
Steven Beranek · personal observation from the pilot

On the pilot, you and your team listened to the actual calls. What did you hear?

On the voice agent realism

"We used to listen to some of the calls. The way they were structured, we were honestly surprised that more customers weren't able to recognize it was AI calling them and not a person. Nobody really said 'hey, is this a robot, are you calling me from AI.' The technology was such that it was kind of seamless from the collector."

"One complaint per three thousand touchpoints. That is unheard of in this industry. From a creditor with reputational risk, that number alone is the case for AgentCollect."
Steve Beranek

"That's the part most people in collections will not believe until they hear it themselves. I didn't fully believe it either, until I sat there and listened."

"AgentCollect is the wave of the future. Anybody not on board is just behind the times."

If this works so well, why hasn't every collection agency built it already?

On why incumbents haven't built this

"AgentCollect is the wave of the future. Anybody not on board is behind the times. The agencies aren't using it today mainly because they can't build it themselves, and they don't want to spend the money."

"Here's the math. Collectors keep about a third of the agency's commission revenue as their pay. If you could convince an agency that AgentCollect replaces those collectors and the profit stays the same, because AgentCollect does the work and the agency keeps the third, they could be convinced. But most don't go down that path. It's a hard internal sell."

If you were running AR for a top 50 company tomorrow, how would you actually use AgentCollect?

How he would deploy it tomorrow

"I'd use AgentCollect on the front end as a filtering mechanism. Every customer goes through it first. AgentCollect resolves what it can resolve. It kicks out the red-flag cases to a live agent."

"And before I wrote off any account, I'd run it through AgentCollect one more time to find any additional contacts. A lost-opportunity check. Because nine times out of ten, that's where the missed money is."
Steve Beranek

Which kinds of operations would benefit most from this?

Where it fits

"Don't limit it. Consumer is much bigger than commercial. I would not stop at B2B."

"At Citibank we had thirty to forty external collection agencies just in the US. Any organization with a serious internal collections operation would benefit from AgentCollect. Every company is looking to do things better and faster. That's the part most people underestimate."

In your own words, what is AgentCollect?

What AgentCollect is, in his words

"AgentCollect is a replacement for an individual collector. It handles mass volume, responds to customers quickly, and finds additional contacts that weren't provided. And it does it in a way that feels seamless. It felt natural to the customer."
Steve Beranek

Closing thought?

The wave

The only real question now is how fast organizations choose to use it -- and anybody not on board is going to be behind the times."

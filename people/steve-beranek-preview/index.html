His career in his words

On his last role at Amazon. "Global bankruptcy and debt recovery. Pretty much anything that was considered late stage collections, or customers that had been written off. Predominantly through the use of third-party collection agencies."

On scale at Citibank and Chase. "We once did a sale where we sold 1.2 billion dollars worth of assets. Probably target of 250 to 300 million dollars a year in collections."

On the team structure. "Internal team was small, five individuals reporting to me. External, we used outside collection agencies and attorneys. At GE Capital, 13 collection centers globally, close to 700 people."

On who pays and who does not. "There are two types of customers that don't pay. The ones that can't pay and the ones that won't pay. The won't pay is really more about resolving the customer's issues."

On Day 90 to 180. "If a customer goes past 90 to 120 days, it becomes much more severe and more than likely they won't pay. That's when it gets to my team, the debt recovery team. We take more aggressive action."

"We listened to the calls. We were honestly surprised. Nobody asked, are you a robot. The technology was seamless enough that it felt like a collector on the other end of the line."
Steve Beranek · on the AgentCollect voice agent

The single most important capability

"Finding the right contact is the most important capability. Without it, nothing happens. The accounts that don't get collected are the ones where the email bounces and the phone is missing. AgentCollect goes out and finds contacts that aren't in your file. A human collector with 200 accounts on their desk doesn't have the time to dig. AgentCollect does."

"If I have the right contact and the customer can pay, I'm more than likely going to collect. AgentCollect finds the contact a human collector never would."
Steve Beranek

Why agencies leave money on the table

"Agencies are paid on dollars collected. So a collector gravitates to the big balances. The small balances get neglected, and the customer on a big balance gets pressured. AgentCollect is indifferent. It doesn't care what the balance is. It treats every account the same. That changes what is recoverable on a portfolio."

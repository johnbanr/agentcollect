Observation 03: three patterns we could not ignore

These three numbers sit inside the 63 debtor messages we read.

Pattern 01 · Invoice visibility
29 of 63 open disputes: "I cannot find this invoice on FedEx Billing Online."
Debtors receive a collections letter referencing FedEx invoice numbers that do not appear in their own portal account. The example we opened first, Lisa Forry at Hanover Foods, searched under every account number for every division of her company. None of the three invoice numbers returned a result.
Pattern 02 · Already paid
12 debtors reported paying FedEx directly between the file hand-off and the first contact from collections.
Check numbers, timestamps, and confirmation language included in the replies. Several attached EFT confirmations and remittance trails. The reconciliation window between FedEx internal AR and the external collections file is wider than the payment window.
Pattern 03 · Internal debtors
9 of the collection sequences carry @fedex.com email addresses and internal cost-center codes.
Examples include "Sn2000 Mct Us2" as debtor company name. These accounts were routed to external collection alongside third-party debtors. They were paused manually before any outreach reached the mailboxes.
"As my local FedEx team could not reconcile in an appropriate timeframe the legitimacy of these invoices, we were billed to these old accounts and as a result causing you all to escalate this to a collections matter which I was hoping to avoid."

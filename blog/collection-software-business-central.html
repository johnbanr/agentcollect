The Business Central Collection Gap

The best collection software for Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central automates past-due invoice recovery with AI-powered outreach that syncs directly with your ERP. If you run your financials on Business Central and need to recover unpaid B2B invoices faster than manual dunning allows, this guide covers the top options in 2026: what they do, how they integrate, and which approach will recover the most cash with the least effort.

Business Central is a powerful ERP for small and mid-market companies, handling everything from general ledger to inventory and purchasing. But its collection capabilities stop at reminder letters and finance charge memos. When a customer ignores your first, second, and third reminder, Business Central gives you no next step. Your AR team is left making manual phone calls, sending one-off emails, and tracking follow-ups on spreadsheets.

This is a particularly acute problem for Business Central users because the platform's sweet spot is growing companies with $5M to $500M in revenue. At this size, you have enough past-due accounts to need automation but not enough AR staff to handle them manually. Every hour your controller spends chasing payments is an hour not spent on financial planning.

What Business Central Offers Natively

Business Central includes two built-in collection features:

Reminders: You can configure up to five reminder levels with different grace periods, fee amounts, and letter text. When you run the "Create Reminders" batch job, Business Central generates reminder documents for overdue customer ledger entries. You then issue them as printed letters or emails.

Finance Charge Memos: These calculate interest on overdue balances and generate formal notices. Useful for enforcing payment terms but ineffective at actually motivating payment.

The limitations are significant:

  • Email only (or print). No voice calls, no SMS, no multi-channel coordination.
  • Batch processing, not real-time. You run the reminder job periodically rather than triggering outreach based on debtor behavior.
  • No intelligence. Same template goes to a Fortune 500 company and a 10-person shop. No personalization, no behavioral triggers.
  • No negotiation. If a customer wants to pay in installments, someone must handle it manually.
  • No escalation path. When reminders fail, there is no automated next step to attorney letters or agency referral.

The AI Agent Approach

For Business Central users, the AI agent approach is particularly compelling because it eliminates the need to hire additional AR staff as you grow. When your Business Central system posts a sales invoice and the customer ledger entry goes past due, the AI agent activates automatically.

The agent researches the debtor company, finds the right decision-maker, and begins a coordinated outreach sequence. If the first email gets no response, the agent calls. If the debtor disputes the invoice, the agent resolves it using data from Business Central. If the debtor needs a payment plan, the agent negotiates within your parameters and records the arrangement.

Push too hard, they fight back. Push too soft, they ghost you. AI finds the exact right pressure for every account.

All activity syncs back to Business Central: payment journal entries, dispute notes, and status updates. Your financial reports stay accurate without anyone manually keying data.

How Integration Works

Business Central offers multiple integration paths:

OData / REST API

Business Central exposes standard and custom API pages via OData v4. Collection software reads posted sales invoices, customer ledger entries, and customer cards, then writes back through API pages or journal entries. This is the most robust approach for real-time bidirectional sync.

AL Extensions

Developers can build AL extensions that run inside Business Central and push data to external collection platforms via HTTP calls. This approach keeps the trigger logic inside the ERP.

File-Based Import

Export your aged trial balance or customer ledger entries as CSV, upload to the collection platform. Simple to start, but manual. Most companies move to API sync within months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Business Central have built-in debt collection?

Business Central includes basic reminder and finance charge memo functionality. You can configure reminder levels with escalating language and fees, but it only sends email or printed letters. It lacks AI voice calls, SMS, payment negotiation, dispute resolution, and behavioral timing. Most companies add dedicated collection software for accounts beyond 30 to 60 days past due.

What is the best collection software for Business Central?

For full AI-powered recovery with zero manual effort, AgentCollect provides dedicated AI agents per account with email, voice, SMS, and attorney escalation. For in-house AR workflow management, tools like Kolleno and iController offer ERP connectors. For native Business Central extensions, search AppSource for AR management add-ons. The best choice depends on volume and whether you want autonomous recovery or team tools.

How does collection software connect to Business Central?

Collection platforms connect via Business Central's OData or REST API endpoints. They read posted sales invoices, customer ledger entries, and customer records, then write back payment journal entries, notes, and status updates. Some tools are available as AppSource extensions installed directly in Business Central.

Can I automate collection reminders in Business Central?

Business Central's Reminder feature lets you configure levels with escalating messages and fees, but they are static templates sent on fixed schedules. Dedicated collection software adds AI personalization, behavioral timing based on open rates, multi-channel outreach, and payment link generation.

How much does collection software for Business Central cost?

AI recovery platforms like AgentCollect charge only when money is recovered, with no monthly fee. SaaS AR tools typically charge $500 to $2,000 per month per user. AppSource extensions range from free to $50 to $200 per user per month. Pricing is indicative and may vary.

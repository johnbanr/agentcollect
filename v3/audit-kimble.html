74 seconds on hold. Zero help provided.
📋 What this reveals
Operator has no billing access. A caller pressing 0 is routed to a general receptionist who can’t look up accounts. The only option is to transfer — and nobody picks up the transfer.
No dedicated AR/billing line in the IVR. The IVR menu covers refuse, limestone, coal, oil and gas — but zero billing option. A customer with an invoice question has no direct path.
No voicemail fallback. When the transfer fails, the caller is left on hold indefinitely. No option to leave a message, no callback. The call ends with zero resolution.

Then we tried to pay online.

The portal exists. It’s linked from the nav bar. But getting in requires something most customers don’t have on hand.

kimblecompanies.com — Unlike many waste management competitors, Kimble does surface “Pay My Bill” in the top navigation. The problem starts once you click it.
jj-portal.kimblecompanies.com — eTower platform, copyright 2016. Requires last invoice number or phone on file. No guest pay. No email lookup.
Contact form — Seven required fields including County. A customer trying to ask about an invoice faces a bureaucratic intake process.

Portal requires last invoice number to register. Customer who loses their invoice has no self-service path. 2016 platform with no guest pay option.

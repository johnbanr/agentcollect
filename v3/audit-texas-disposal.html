What we found.

Four friction points. Each one costs Texas Disposal Systems revenue it should already have.

01

7 minutes on the phone. Account not found. Call ends by inactivity.

The single most damaging finding. Mike from Summit Services called to pay a bill. The agent could not locate the account after 7 minutes and 21 seconds of searching. The call ended by inactivity — no payment collected, no callback arranged, no next step. This is not an edge case. It is what happens when an account name has even slight variations from what’s in the system, and there is no manual override or escalation path.

✗ Critical
02

Payment portal on a separate domain, two credentials required, Soft-Pak 2016

tdspayments.com is a third-party Soft-Pak platform last updated in 2016. A customer must have both a Customer Number and an Access Code to reach the payment screen — credentials most customers do not have readily available. There are no instructions on the portal for how to obtain them, no guest payment option, and no alternative path forward if the credentials are lost.

✗ High friction
03

Contact page is a locations directory. Zero email contact. Zero form.

A customer who cannot get through by phone and is blocked at the portal has nowhere else to turn. The contact page lists physical addresses and phone numbers only. There is no billing email, no contact form, and no online support option. A cookie consent banner also obscures the page on every load, adding yet another layer of friction before any useful information is visible.

⚠ Gap
04

Hiring bonus modal fires on every homepage load

A full-screen modal advertising a hiring bonus appears on every visit to the homepage — for every visitor, regardless of intent. Customers arriving to find their billing link are greeted with a recruitment advertisement. This is targeted at job seekers but fires indiscriminately, creating friction for every paying customer at the first moment of their visit.

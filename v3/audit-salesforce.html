Key Findings

critical Salesforce Has No Phone-Based Billing Support

All 5 calls to Salesforce (800) 667-6389 ended the same way: a recorded message directing callers to billing.salesforce.com. There is no option to speak with a human. Pressing 3 for billing plays a recording and hangs up. A customer who wants to pay by phone simply cannot.

irony Salesforce Recommends "Agentforce" on Their Billing Line

When you press 3 for billing, the recorded message says: "Please log in to our portal at billing.salesforce.com, where you can get assistance from Agentforce." Salesforce's own AI agent only exists behind a login wall on a portal. On the phone? A 47-second recorded message that ends with "Have a great day." No agent. No human. Nothing.

critical IVR System Crashed Mid-Call

On call #4, after failing to recognize DTMF inputs 3 times, the Salesforce IVR played: "We're sorry. An error has occurred. Please try back again later." The system crashed entirely. No fallback, no transfer to operator, no callback option.

critical Broken Voice Recognition Creates Infinite Loops

The IVR repeatedly failed to recognize button presses, playing "Sorry. We did not recognize your response" up to 3 times in a row before either crashing or playing the billing recording. DTMF tones were sent correctly by our system.

high Salesforce Uses Radius Global Solutions for Collections

Confirmed via 3 independent BBB complaints: Salesforce sends delinquent accounts to Radius Global Solutions (Edina, MN). Radius has 579 BBB complaints in 3 years and a 1.08/5 consumer rating. Their phone number is (888) 287-5711. Mystery shop calls in progress.

high Spanish Caller Stuck on Silent Hold for 5 Minutes

A Spanish-speaking caller to Radius Global Solutions pressed 0 for an operator and was placed on hold in complete silence. No hold music, no estimated wait time, no periodic reassurance. After 5 full minutes of dead air, the call was terminated by our system hitting the maximum duration limit. No one ever picked up.

high No Spanish Language Support Anywhere

Neither Salesforce nor Radius Global Solutions offered any Spanish language option. Our Spanish-speaking caller asked "Hay alguien que hable espanol?" on multiple calls and received only silence or English-only automated responses. For a company operating in the US market, this is a significant accessibility gap.

medium No Real-Time Dispute Resolution Path

Customers who want to dispute a charge have no phone-based path to resolution. Salesforce's billing line directs to a portal. Radius Global Solutions goes to voicemail. A customer with a legitimate complaint about a $450 bill has no way to speak to anyone in real time.

irony Salesforce Sells $50/seat/mo Collection Software

Salesforce Revenue Cloud includes collections management at $50/user/month. Yet their own AR operation sends delinquent accounts to Radius Global Solutions, a third-party collection agency with 579 BBB complaints in 3 years and a 1.08/5 consumer rating. Their billing phone line is a recorded message. No human. No AI agent. Just "log in to the portal."

Radius Global Solutions Collection Portal

Salesforce outsources overdue AR to Radius Global Solutions. Here's what their online payment experience looks like:

high WordPress-Based Payment Portal

Radius Global Solutions' payment portal (paymentportal.radiusgs.com) is the page Salesforce's delinquent customers land on. Radius has 579 BBB complaints in 3 years and a 1.08/5 consumer rating. This is the collections experience for a $300B CRM company.

medium Visa Debit Only

The payment portal only accepts Visa debit cards. No Mastercard, no Amex, no ACH, no credit cards. This severely limits a debtor's ability to pay and creates unnecessary friction in the resolution process.

medium Maximum 3 Installments

Payment plans are limited to a maximum of 3 installments. For larger balances, this creates hardship and reduces the likelihood of successful recovery.

the most important essay on capital i've ever read was written in 2000 by mike milken.

i read it the week before YC.

one line saved my startup's positioning:

"this bias toward the past, what i call financing in the rear-view mirror, systematically excluded companies that were the actual engines of growth."

25 years later, collection agencies operate identically:

1. they score debtors on past delinquency (rear-view)
2. they refuse sub-$5k invoices as "uncollectable"
3. they miss the forward signal: debtor is still operating, still paying other vendors, still hiring
4. result: us companies write off around $680b/yr in invoices that are actually collectable

i spent a weekend writing about the parallel this week. link in comments.

he didn't reply. still no idea why :)

What is Skip Tracing in B2B Debt Collection?

Skip tracing is the process of locating a debtor who has become unreachable — finding their current contact information, decision-makers, or business address.

Definition

Skip Tracing Explained

Skip tracing is the process of locating a debtor or the correct contact at a debtor company when existing contact information is outdated, wrong, or unavailable. The term comes from "skip" — someone who has left without paying — and "trace" — finding where they went. In practice, skip tracing means finding the right phone number, verified email address, current business address, or decision-maker name.

In consumer debt collection, skip tracing typically means finding a specific individual who has moved or changed their phone number. In B2B, the challenge is different and often more nuanced: the company still exists, but you do not know who the right person is. Is it the AP Specialist? The Controller? The CFO? Finding the right contact with actual authority to approve payment — and verifying their direct line — is the B2B version of skip tracing.

Skip tracing is the prerequisite for effective collection. Sending a demand to the wrong email, calling a disconnected number, or addressing a letter to a former employee wastes time and kills recovery rates. Before any outreach can succeed, you need accurate, verified contact data for the specific person who can authorize payment.

Key Points

What You Need to Know About B2B Skip Tracing

  • Finding the AP Specialist is not enough. In B2B, effective skip tracing builds an escalation chain: AP Specialist → Controller → CFO. When the AP contact ignores you, you need the next contact ready immediately.
  • Phone lookup is the highest-value action. A verified direct dial number for the right decision-maker delivers 4–6x higher response rates than email alone. Skip tracing that does not include phone verification is incomplete.
  • Email verification prevents deliverability damage. Sending to bad email addresses increases your bounce rate and harms the sender reputation of your domain. Always verify emails before adding them to outreach sequences.
  • LinkedIn decision-maker research is standard practice. For mid-to-large companies, LinkedIn reveals the current org structure — who is actually in the AP, Finance, and CFO roles today, not 18 months ago.
  • Business address updates matter for attorney escalation. If legal action is needed, the attorney needs a verified current business address for service of process. Stale addresses invalidate demand letters and slow litigation.
B2B vs. Consumer

How B2B Skip Tracing Differs from Consumer

The Core Difference
Consumer: find the person  |  B2B: find the right person at the company

In consumer collections, the debtor and the responsible party are the same individual. Skip tracing means locating them physically — new address, new phone number, new employer.

In B2B, the company still exists and you know where they are. The skip tracing challenge is organizational: who inside that company has the authority and the responsibility to pay this invoice? The AP Specialist who opened the account may have left. The Controller may have changed. The CFO may have been replaced in a recent acquisition. B2B skip tracing is as much about org chart research as it is about contact data lookup.

Example

Skip Tracing in Practice: B2B Example

Scenario: $40K Invoice, AP Contact Gone Dark

Situation: A staffing agency is owed $40,000 from a client. Their AP contact, Sarah M., left the company 3 months ago. Emails bounce. The main company number rings with no answer.

Skip tracing process: LinkedIn search reveals the new AP Manager (Jason T.) and Controller (Maria R.). Phone lookup returns Jason's direct dial. Email verification confirms jason.t@company.com is active. Controller email added as escalation backup.

Outreach result: First call to Jason's direct line — answered on the second attempt. Invoice approved for payment within 14 days. No agency needed. No legal escalation required.

Without skip tracing: All outreach goes to a bouncing email address and a dead phone number. Account ages into 120-day-past-due territory. Recovery rate drops from ~95% to ~60%. Skip tracing was the difference between collecting in full and writing off 40% of the balance.

Data Sources

Common Skip Tracing Tools and Data Sources

LinkedIn
Org chart research
Current role verification, decision-maker identification, direct connection to AP and Finance contacts.
Apollo / ZoomInfo
Contact databases
Direct dial phone numbers, verified work emails, company org chart data at scale.
USPS NCOA
Address validation
National Change of Address database. Verifies and updates business mailing addresses.

Most professional AR teams use a combination of sources — no single provider has complete, current data for every company. Cross-referencing two independent sources (e.g., Apollo email + LinkedIn role verification) before outreach is the standard for quality skip tracing.

FAQ

Skip Tracing FAQ

What does skip tracing cost for B2B debt collection?
Skip tracing costs vary widely: manual research (LinkedIn, public records) can be done internally at the cost of staff time; commercial data providers like ZoomInfo or Apollo charge $500–$5,000/month for platform access; per-record services typically run $0.10–$2.00 per contact depending on data quality and depth. For most B2B use cases, using an enrichment platform that auto-runs skip tracing at the start of every collection campaign is the most cost-effective approach.
Is skip tracing legal in B2B debt collection?
Yes — skip tracing using publicly available information (LinkedIn, business registries, USPS NCOA, public court records, professional databases) is fully legal in B2B collections. The legal line is drawn at methods involving deception (pretexting — pretending to be someone else to extract contact information) or accessing private systems without authorization. Always use licensed data providers and ensure your skip tracing practices comply with applicable state laws.

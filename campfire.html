-- The pattern --

AI has automated the close at both. The chase before cash arrives is still one human, one inbox, polite email then a louder one.

Campfire's case studies (and the Series B PR) are clear about the scope: revenue close, journal entries, reconciliation, anomaly flagging. That's a real category and we're rooting for it. What's not in those case studies, and what we couldn't find anywhere in the public footprint of either customer, is anything that automates the gap between "invoice issued" and "cash in bank" on a past-due account. That's the gap.

-- Where we pick up --

We don't replace Campfire. We extend the loop.

Campfire closes the books. We collect the cash that should have already been in them. We start at past-due, end at cash-in-bank, and we do it on phone, SMS, and email at the same time -- not just one. The loop runs end to end and reports the result back to whatever system of record the customer is on (which is increasingly Campfire).

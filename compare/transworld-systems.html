The Real Difference

The commission-based model has structural misalignments

Traditional agencies like Transworld Systems have been around for decades, and their scale is real. But the commission-based human-collector model has built-in constraints that technology has now made unnecessary.

Human collectors are expensive, inconsistent, and capacity-constrained. They can only work certain hours, they have good days and bad days, and their commission incentive means they focus on the easiest accounts first — leaving harder cases untouched. When a collector calls from "Transworld Systems" rather than from your company name, the debtor immediately knows the relationship has moved to collections, which can harden their position rather than resolve the account.

AgentCollect's AI handles hundreds of accounts simultaneously, makes first contact the same day an account is uploaded, and conducts every call under your company's name. When human judgment is truly needed — on contested accounts, unusual situations, or legal escalation — our attorney network steps in. This combination typically produces faster results at lower cost, while protecting the customer relationships that matter most to your business.

Who Switches

  • A client complained about the agency calls. Receiving a call from "Transworld Systems" told a customer — often one who was just slow to pay — that they'd been sent to collections. The relationship damage often exceeded the recovered amount.

  • Results came too slowly. Traditional agency workflows involve account assignment queues, collector availability, and escalation approvals. AI works all accounts simultaneously from day one.

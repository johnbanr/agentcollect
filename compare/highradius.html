The Real Difference

Automation optimizes. Collection closes.

HighRadius is genuinely excellent at what it does: automating the AR process, prioritizing collector queues, cash application, credit management, and deduction management. For large enterprises handling thousands of accounts, that automation creates real efficiency gains.

But here's the gap: HighRadius helps your team work smarter on AR — it doesn't replace the need to actively collect from accounts that aren't paying. Automated dunning emails and payment reminders move the easy accounts. The ones that are 90+ days past due, involve disputes, or where the contact has gone silent — those need something automated dunning can't provide: a real conversation, escalating pressure, and ultimately the credible threat of legal action.

AgentCollect is designed specifically for this late-stage collection gap. HighRadius customers typically export aged AR (90+, 120+, 180+ days) to AgentCollect, where AI voice agents make direct contact, present payment options, and escalate to attorney-backed demand letters when needed. The two platforms are highly complementary — HighRadius handles the process, AgentCollect handles the recovery.

Who Switches

HighRadius customers add AgentCollect when…

  • Automated dunning isn't closing the gap. HighRadius optimized the dunning process but a meaningful percentage of the AR book still sits aged. Automation moved the easy accounts; the hard ones need active collection.
  • The collections team is overwhelmed. HighRadius helps prioritize which accounts the team should work, but if there aren't enough collectors to work all the aged accounts, many go untouched. AgentCollect autonomously works every account simultaneously.
  • They need attorney escalation without a third-party agency. HighRadius doesn't offer attorney-backed demand letters. When accounts need that escalation, HighRadius customers typically need to bring in an outside agency — AgentCollect provides the attorney layer without the full agency relationship or 30%+ fees.

The Real Difference

Analytics vs. action: fundamentally different products

Prodigal has analyzed over 500 million consumer finance interactions and built a strong AI intelligence platform for the consumer lending industry. Their technology helps collection agencies understand what happens on calls, identify compliance risks, and coach human agents to perform better. For consumer finance companies with large call centers, Prodigal provides genuine value.

But Prodigal does not make calls, send emails, write demand letters, or collect payments. It is an analytics layer that sits on top of existing collection operations. It tells you what happened. It does not do the collecting.

AgentCollect is the opposite. It IS the collection agent. When you upload your AR file, AgentCollect deploys a dedicated AI agent for your account that researches the debtor company, enriches contact information (Contact Finder increases reach by up to 130%), and launches coordinated outreach across email sequences (attorney mode achieves 70% open rates), SMS campaigns, AI voice calls, and formal demand letters through licensed US attorneys in all 50 states. Debtors can pay directly through a self-service payment portal the same day they are contacted. Disputes are resolved 90% of the time without human intervention.

Additionally, Prodigal focuses on consumer finance (personal loans, auto lending, healthcare billing). AgentCollect is built exclusively for B2B commercial debt, where the debtor is a business and the invoices are significantly larger. These are different markets with different dynamics.

Who Switches

Companies choose AgentCollect over Prodigal when...

  • They need a collector, not an analyst. Prodigal helps you understand your existing collection operations. AgentCollect replaces or augments those operations entirely. If you want AI to do the collecting, not just analyze it, AgentCollect is the right tool.
  • They want to eliminate collection headcount. Prodigal requires human agents to make the actual calls and send the actual communications. AgentCollect handles the full collection cycle autonomously, with each client receiving a dedicated AI agent.
  • Their debt is B2B commercial. Prodigal's models are trained on consumer finance interactions. B2B commercial debt has different dynamics: AP departments instead of individuals, purchase orders instead of loan agreements, and professional escalation paths including attorney demand letters.
  • They want success-only pricing. Prodigal charges SaaS licensing fees regardless of collection outcomes. AgentCollect charges only when money is recovered, with no upfront costs or minimums.

What is the difference between Prodigal and AgentCollect?
Prodigal is an intelligence and analytics layer that analyzes existing collection calls and interactions to surface insights, compliance risks, and agent coaching opportunities. It does not contact debtors. AgentCollect IS the collection agent. It contacts debtors across email, SMS, voice, and demand letters, negotiates payment, and processes collection through a direct payment portal.
Can I use Prodigal and AgentCollect together?
They solve different problems. Prodigal analyzes calls made by human agents or other systems. AgentCollect replaces or augments human agents by handling outreach, negotiation, and payment collection autonomously. If you have human agents handling some accounts and want analytics, you could use Prodigal on those. For accounts you want fully automated collection, use AgentCollect.
Does AgentCollect include call analytics like Prodigal?
AgentCollect provides a real-time dashboard with full visibility into every interaction (calls, emails, SMS, letters) with outcomes, payment status, and dispute resolution. While AgentCollect does not position itself as a standalone analytics product, all collection intelligence is built into the platform.
Is Prodigal built for B2B commercial debt?
Prodigal focuses on consumer finance (personal loans, auto lending, healthcare billing). AgentCollect is built exclusively for B2B commercial debt where the debtor is a business, the invoices are larger, and the collection approach requires professional escalation and attorney involvement.
